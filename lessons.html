Lessons

Lesson 1: The Present Simple

We use the present simple to describe things that are always ture, or situation that exist now and, as far as we know, will go on indefinitely.

Ex: The sun always rises in the east

Lesson 2: The Present Continuous

We use the present continuous to imply that a situation is or may be temporary.

Ex: Marie is teaching mathematics at a local school

We also use the present continuous to talk about changes, developments, and trends.

Ex: I'm beginning to realise how difficult it is to be a father

Lesson 3: The Past Simple

When we want to indicate that something happened at a specific time in the past, we use the past simple.

Ex: She left juse a few minutes ago

Lesson 4: The Present Perfect

We use the present perfect when we talk about something that happened in a period of time up to the present. As oppose to the past simple where we use it to talk about somthing that happened at a particular, finished time in the past.

Ex: Tom has taken a lot of medication.

Lesson 5: The Past Continuous

We use the past continuous to talk about a temporary situation that existed at or around a particular time in the past.

Ex: I was talking to someone a moment ago.
